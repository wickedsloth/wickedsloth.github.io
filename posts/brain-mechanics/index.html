Brain Mechanics

Fri 09 March 2018

From the reading of "Brain at Work" by David Rock which I recommend.

Facts about the brain

  • Prefrontal cortex can hold a surprisingly small numbers of information at the same time
  • Focus on a single task at a time

Hence, one needs to be using its brain capacity wisely. Multitasking, or jumping from one task to another too frequently is generally inefficient.

  • Over and under arousal are both detrimental to mental performance. It is important to have an "overseer mind" to be aware of one's mental state

Handling Negative Emotional Responses

  • Labelling the feeling
  • Reappraisal: changing the view of the event and find ways to create a narrative of choice and autonomy whenever possible

Expectations

  • The brain is sensitive to gap to expectation
  • Negative gap to expectation is more significant than positive gap to expectation
  • Always keep in mind to manage expectations. The outcome of underpromising is always better than overpromising
  • Same goes to self expectation setting

SCARF model

The scarf model describes main rewards or threats important to the brain.

  • Status
  • Certainty
  • Autonomy
  • Relatedness
  • Fairness

Persuading another person

  • Providing feedback is often counterproductive (sense of threat)
  • Providing solution can be equally inefficient (lowering of the status)
  • Instead, try asking questions and stir the partner to find its own answers (Socrates' maieutic)

Changing the culture

There are two types of goals: towards and away goals. Away goals are negative in nature ("lose weight", "stop smoking"), towards goals are target centered and are more beneficial.

  • Change is often viewed as threat
  • Need to focus attention on towards goals and reactivate them on a regular basis
  • Use the SCARF model
